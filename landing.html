A-EYE Glasses combine computer vision, distance sensing, and intelligent audio feedback to empower visually impaired users with real-time awareness. Beyond barcode decoding, the system detects nearby and distant obstacles, recognizes texts and objects, and analyzes full visual scenes to describe situations through natural speech - giving users eyes-free understanding of their surroundings.

Close-range obstacle detection

Detects obstacles located near the user’s upper body using a short-range distance sensor, providing immediate alerts for nearby hazards such as tables, shelves, or people.

Long-range obstacle detection

Utilizes a long-range sensor to identify obstacles several meters ahead, enabling safe navigation and proactive awareness of upcoming barriers.

Real-time text reading (Text-to-Speech)

Reads any written text visible in front of the glasses - such as signs, labels, or menus - using optical recognition and instant voice narration through the built-in speaker.

Object identification (Photo-to-Text-to-Speech)

Recognizes everyday objects captured by the camera and verbally announces their names and categories, assisting users in understanding their surroundings.

Scene understanding and description

Analyzes captured images to describe the full visual scene - identifying people, objects, and actions - allowing the user to hear a spoken explanation of what’s happening in front of them.

How A-EYE Glasses work

The A-EYE Glasses integrate computer vision, distance sensing, and intelligent voice feedback to guide visually impaired users safely and independently. Every component - from the camera to the laser sensors - collaborates in real time to interpret the environment and communicate it clearly through sound.

1 · Sense & detect

Short- and long-range sensors continuously scan the user’s surroundings, identifying obstacles in both the near and far field to prevent collisions with the upper body.

2 · Capture & analyze

The built-in camera captures the environment and analyzes it with AI to recognize texts, barcodes, objects, and full visual scenes, providing rich contextual understanding.

3 · Describe & guide

Text, barcodes, and visual insights are instantly translated into speech through the onboard speaker, so users hear real-time descriptions and navigate confidently.
